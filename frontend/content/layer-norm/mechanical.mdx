## How It Works
Given an activation vector x of dimension d, LayerNorm computes μ = mean(x), σ² = var(x), then outputs γ ⊙ (x - μ)/√(σ² + ε) + β. The learnable parameters γ and β allow the model to rescale and shift the normalized output. The small constant ε prevents division by zero.

## Key Components
Pre-LayerNorm (applied before attention/FFN) is more stable than Post-LayerNorm (applied after) and is used in most modern LLMs. RMSNorm is a simplified variant that omits the mean subtraction, computing only the RMS normalization: x / RMS(x) · γ. RMSNorm is faster and used in LLaMA, Mistral, and other efficient models.

## Step by Step
Compute the mean and variance of the input vector across its feature dimension. Subtract the mean and divide by the standard deviation (plus epsilon). Multiply by the learned scale γ and add the learned bias β to produce the normalized output.
