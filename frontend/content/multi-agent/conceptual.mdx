## What It Is
Multi-agent systems consist of multiple LLM-powered agents that collaborate, communicate, and divide work to accomplish complex tasks. Each agent can have a specialized role, different tools, or a different model. Agents coordinate through message passing, shared memory, or orchestration by a central controller.

## Core Idea
Complex tasks often benefit from specialization and parallelism — just as human teams divide work among specialists. In a multi-agent system, a planner agent breaks down a task, specialist agents execute subtasks, and a critic agent reviews results. This division of labor can produce higher quality results than a single generalist agent.

## Why It Matters
Multi-agent systems can tackle tasks too complex for a single agent's context window or capabilities. They enable parallelism, with multiple agents working simultaneously on independent subtasks. Frameworks like AutoGen, CrewAI, and LangGraph have made multi-agent orchestration accessible for production applications.
