## What It Is
Reflection is a prompting and agentic technique where an LLM critiques and revises its own previous output. The model is prompted to identify errors, inconsistencies, or improvements in its initial response and then produce a refined version. It is a form of iterative self-improvement without external feedback.

## Core Idea
LLMs often produce better outputs when given the opportunity to review and revise rather than generating in a single pass. Reflection leverages the model's ability to evaluate text (which is often stronger than its ability to generate perfectly on the first try) to improve its own outputs. This mirrors how humans draft, review, and revise their work.

## Why It Matters
Reflection can significantly improve output quality on tasks like code generation, essay writing, and complex reasoning without any additional training. It is a key component of agentic systems where the model must self-correct after tool failures or incorrect reasoning. It enables models to catch their own mistakes before presenting a final answer.
